# Cooking Techniques

## Tempering (tadka)

- Heat oil or ghee; add whole spices until fragrant or seeds pop.
- Pour over cooked dal or yogurt dishes for aroma.

## Pressure cooking

- Dal and legumes: cook with turmeric and water; natural release safer for foaming foods.
- Rice: water ratio depends on rice type; often 1:2 for basmati in cooker.

## Sautéing base masala

- Onions until golden; add ginger-garlic; tomatoes until oil separates.
- This base works for many curries before adding main ingredient.

## Slow simmer (dum)

- Seal pot with dough or tight lid; low heat for biryani and meat finishing.
- Rest 10 minutes before opening to keep steam in.

## Steaming

- Idli molds greased lightly; 10 to 12 minutes after batter is fermented.
- Vegetables: minimal water loss; keeps nutrients.

## Deep frying safety

- Dry foods before frying to reduce splatter.
- Do not overcrowd pan; oil temperature drops if too full.

## Food safety temperatures

- Chicken and poultry: fully cooked, juices run clear, 165 degrees Fahrenheit internal.
- Reheat leftovers until steaming hot throughout.

## Storing cooked food

- Cool quickly in shallow containers; refrigerate within 2 hours.
- Most cooked dishes keep 2 to 3 days refrigerated.
