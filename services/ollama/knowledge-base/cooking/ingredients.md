# Ingredients and Substitutions

## Common pantry staples

- Rice: basmati for pulao and biryani; sona masoor or toor dal for everyday dal.
- Lentils: toor, moong, masoor, chana — soaking times vary; pressure cooker saves time.
- Flours: whole wheat for roti and paratha; refined flour for bhature and some sweets.

## Dairy in Indian cooking

- Ghee: clarified butter; high smoke point; temper spices in ghee.
- Paneer: fresh cheese; does not melt; grills and curries well.
- Yogurt: marinades, raita, kadhi; curdle if boiled rapidly — stir in on low heat.
- Cream: finish rich gravies; add at end with heat off or very low.

## Vegetables — prep basics

- Onion, tomato, ginger-garlic: base for most North Indian gravies.
- Potato: boiling, mashing, stuffing paratha, pav bhaji.
- Spinach: blanch for palak dishes; squeeze water for saag.

## Substitutions

- Heavy cream: coconut milk in curries, or milk plus butter.
- Buttermilk: milk plus lemon juice, rest 5 minutes.
- Egg in baking: flax meal and water mix per egg.
- Lemon juice: amchur for dry tangy note in some dishes.

## Oil choices

- Mustard oil: traditional East Indian flavor; heat until smoking point then cool slightly.
- Sunflower or neutral oil: everyday frying and sautéing.
- Coconut oil: South Indian and coastal flavors.

## Measuring for home cooking

- Teaspoon and tablespoon standard; cups for rice and flour by volume.
- Weighing spices improves consistency when learning new recipes.
