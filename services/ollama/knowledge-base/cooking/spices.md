# Spices and Seasonings

## Turmeric (haldi)

- Warm, earthy yellow spice; key in curries and rice.
- Use in dry form; blooms in hot oil or ghee. Pairs with cumin, coriander, and chili.
- Stains surfaces; add early in cooking for depth.

## Cumin (jeera)

- Whole seeds: temper in hot oil until fragrant for dals and vegetables.
- Ground cumin: earthy, nutty; good in raita, marinades, and spice blends.
- Whole and ground are not always interchangeable in equal amounts.

## Coriander (dhania)

- Seeds: citrusy, floral when ground; base of many Indian masalas.
- Fresh leaves: garnish curries, chutneys, and salads; add at end of cooking.
- Ground coriander is milder than fresh leaves.

## Red chili powder (lal mirch)

- Heat level varies by brand; start small and adjust.
- Kashmiri chili: more color, less heat. Regular chili powder is hotter.
- Store airtight away from light.

## Garam masala

- Warm blend: often cardamom, cinnamon, cloves, cumin, coriander.
- Add near end of cooking to keep aroma; can also sprinkle on finished dishes.
- Homemade blends toast whole spices then grind.

## Mustard seeds (rai)

- Black mustard: sharp; pop in hot oil until they splutter.
- Yellow mustard: milder; common in pickles and tempering.
- Never substitute ground mustard 1:1 for whole seeds in tempering.

## Cardamom (elaichi)

- Green: sweet, floral; desserts, chai, biryani, kheer.
- Black: smoky; savory dishes and some garam masala blends.
- Crush pods lightly to release flavor; remove pods before serving if preferred.

## Cinnamon (dalchini)

- Stick: slow-release sweetness; biryanis, pulao, and meat curries.
- Powder: stronger; use less than stick; common in baking and masala chai.

## Cloves (laung)

- Intense, warm; use sparingly. Whole in rice and meat dishes.
- Ground cloves are very strong; a pinch is often enough.

## Fenugreek (methi)

- Seeds: bitter if overused; common in pickles and spice mixes.
- Dried leaves (kasuri methi): crush and add to curries, paratha, and sauces at the end.

## Pav bhaji masala

- Blend for pav bhaji: typically coriander, cumin, chili, amchur, and kasuri methi.
- Add while sautéing vegetables for the bhaji.

## Sambar powder

- Used in South Indian sambar; tangy, spiced lentil stew flavor.
- Add with vegetables and tamarind in sambar; adjust salt and tamarind to taste.

## Amchur (dry mango powder)

- Sour, fruity tang without liquid; chaats, chutneys, and dry vegetable dishes.
- Good substitute hint when lemon is unavailable in dry preparations.

## Kasuri methi usage tip

- Lightly crush dried fenugreek leaves between palms before adding.
- Enhances butter chicken, dal, and restaurant-style curries.
